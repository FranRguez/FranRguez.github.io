Descriptive analysis about theDataMap healthcare database



One of the data breaches accounted for the 67,7% percent of total records breached in our time period.
In order to better analyse the rest, in some analysis this breach has been excluded (those are marked with *).
Insurers accounted for the 52,7% of all data records breached.* However, it represented 20% of the total number of breaches reported, while discharge data was 39%.
This could be explained because of the higher amount of information insurers deal with.

US Central Government has been the most affected organization, with the 75% percent of the total records breached.
Although it only accounted for 1.2% of total number of data breaches reports. If we exclude this organization, how was the distribution per state?

Analysis of health data breaches, by state*

As you can see, California, Texas and Maryland are the most affected states, accounting for 37% of total reported*, mainly affecting the private sector.

Conclusions

- US Central Government has received the highest impact of all organizations reporting data breaches from 2005 to 2017.
- California, Texas and Maryland have reported 37% of the breaches.
- Insurers are shown to be specially vulnerable organizations.
- Data breaches will probably increase in the next decade. In this visualization, we try to provide tools to prioritize and foster further actions to protect patients' health data.
